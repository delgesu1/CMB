The Musicians

Our Artists

Exceptional musicians and presenters dedicated to bringing world-class chamber music to the Treasure Valley.

Violinist

Rosie Weiss

Rosie Weiss is a versatile violinist, fiddler, and studio musician. She currently serves as associate concertmaster of the Billings Symphony and performs with the Boise Philharmonic, Bozeman Symphony, and Palm Beach Symphony.

As a founding member of Carnegie Hall's National Youth Orchestra, she served for three seasons as assistant and associate concertmaster, performing across Russia, Europe, and China. She has shared the stage with conductors including Valery Gergiev, Charles Dutoit, and Gerard Schwarz, and artists such as Joshua Bell, Emanuel Ax, and Daniil Trifonov.

As a chamber musician, Rosie has performed at Festival Napa Valley, Zephyr International Chamber Music Festival, and National Sawdust in New York. Most recently, she joined Thirdspace Quartet for a full season in Boise. Her recording credits include a Grammy award-winning album by John Daversa.

In her free time, you can find Rosie hiking and birding in her favorite places in Idaho and Montana.

Violinist

Nicole Oswald

Nicole Oswald is the newly appointed Associate Concertmaster of the Boise Philharmonic Orchestra and currently acting as Concertmaster. She is Artist in Residence at the College of Idaho and violinist in the Langroise Trio.

Previously, Nicole was a tenured core member of the Virginia Symphony Orchestra, and prior to that served as concertmaster of The Orchestra Now and a member of the West Virginia Symphony Orchestra. She has collaborated with artists including Gil Shaham, Andrés Cárdenes, Charlie Castleman, and members of the Bergonzi String Quartet.

As an educator, Nicole maintains a private studio, coaches chamber music, and works with youth music organizations and public school programs. She has been invited to perform and teach at the College of William and Mary, Old Dominion University, and Christopher Newport University, and was faculty at Miami Youth for Chamber Music.

Ms. Oswald attended the Eastman School of Music (BM), Frost School of Music (Artist Diploma), and Carnegie Mellon University (Master of Music). As a teenager, she studied abroad for three years at the Utrechts Conservatory in The Netherlands.

Beyond the concert hall, Nicole is dedicated to music education and community engagement, believing that chamber music has the power to create meaningful connections between performers and audiences.

Violist

Aubrey Holmes

Violinist and violist Aubrey Holmes is currently a member of the Boise Philharmonic's viola section. During the 2022/2023 season, he was a full-time core member of the Virginia Symphony Orchestra's violin section, rotating into Assistant Concertmaster.

An avid chamber musician, Aubrey is a founding member and violinist of the Heimat Quartet (2014-2020), which gave their Carnegie Hall debut in 2019. The quartet joined the faculty at Shenandoah Conservatory and were quarter-finalists at both the 2017 Fischoff National Chamber Music Competition and the 2019 Joachim International Chamber Music Competition in Weimar, Germany.

International appearances include the International Mendelssohn Festival in Hamburg, Germany, The Esmeraldas International Music Festival in Quito, Ecuador, and the Lunenberg Academy of Music in Nova Scotia. From 2011-2015, Mr. Holmes was based in Boston, serving as Concertmaster/Principal 2nd with Symphony Nova and performing with MetroWest Opera, Symphony Pro Musica, and Boston Opera Collaborative.

Mr. Holmes' primary mentors have included Dr. Blanka Bednarz, Dr. Irina Muresanu, Cathy Meng Robinson, and Daniel Kurganov, as well as members of the Miami String Quartet, Shanghai Quartet, and the Audubon Quartet.

Aubrey's infectious enthusiasm for music extends beyond performance to education and outreach, where he works to inspire the next generation of chamber musicians.

Presenters

Presenter

Bradley Berg

Bradley Berg is a pianist, historian, presenter, and performing arts advocate. He holds a degree from Indiana University and brings deep expertise in classical music history and performance to his work.

By day, Bradley serves as Grants Manager for the Boise Philharmonic. By evening, he's a musicologist and Boise State Public Radio Music host. He joined Boise State Public Radio Music as host of the Boise Philharmonic Showcase in 2023—the perfect complement to his administrative role, where as Musicologist he meets the world-class musicians, guest artists, composers, and conductors who make their way through Boise.

The Showcase aims to capture and comment on the artistic vibrancy of Boise within the realm of classical music. Through interviews with touring artists and curated playlists that reflect local programming, Bradley showcases Boise as Idaho's cultural hub for the performing arts. He also runs a YouTube channel dedicated to original performances of rare and previously unrecorded classical piano music.

Presenter

Karen Mang Kennedy

Karen hails from Saskatchewan and completed her Bachelor of Music Performance (clarinet) at the University of Victoria, where she toured extensively with the UVic Chamber Singers.

She served in the Royal Canadian Mounted Police for 13 years in North Vancouver, Headquarters, and Surrey, BC. Karen has sung with the Vancouver Chamber Choir, musica intima vocal ensemble, and as section leader with Christ Church Cathedral Choir in Vancouver.

Before immigrating to Boise, she worked as a freelance musician in the Vancouver area. Having sung for the Heath Arts Society in BC, she is excited to bring this concept of music outreach to residential care homes in Idaho.
